Chicken Biryani

Ingrediants:

For the rice

  • Basmati rice - 2 cups
  • Water-4 cups
  • Salt - 1 tsp
  • Bay leaf - 1
  • Green cardamom - 3
  • Cloves - 3
  • Cinnamon stick - 1 small piece

For chicken marinade

  • Chicken - 500 g (cut into pieces)
  • Yogurt - ½ cup
  • Ginger-garlic paste - 2 tsp
  • Red chili powder - 1 tsp
  • Turmeric powder - ½ tsp
  • Garam masala - 1 tsp
  • Lemon juice - 1 tbsp
  • Salt - 1 tsp

For the Biryani Gravy

  • Onions - 2 large (thinly sliced)
  • Tomatoes - 2 medium (chopped)
  • Green chilies - 2 (slit)
  • Ginger-garlic paste - 1 tsp
  • Biryani masala - 1 tsp
  • Fresh coriander - 2 tbsp (chopped)
  • Fresh mint leaves - 2 tbsp (chopped)
  • Oil or ghee - 3 tbsp

Optional toppings:

  • Fried onions - ½ cup
  • Saffron - a few strands (soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk)
  • Cashews and raisins - a few for garnish

Insrtuctions

Step 1: Marinate the chicken

  1. Mix chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, lemon juice, and salt.
  2. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour (overnight is best for flavor).

Step 2: Cook the rice

  1. Wash basmati rice thoroughly.
  2. Boil water with salt, bay leaf, cardamom, cloves, and cinnamon.
  3. Add rice and cook until 70% done (rice should still have a bite).
  4. Drain water and set rice aside.

Step 3: Prepare the chicken gravy

  1. Heat oil/ghee in a deep pan.
  2. Fry sliced onions until golden brown. Remove half for garnishing.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for 1-2 minutes.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes, biryani masala, salt, and cook until oil separates.
  5. Add marinated chicken and cook for 10-15 minutes until chicken is almost done.
  6. Mix in chopped coriander and mint leaves.

Step 4: Layering for Dum (slow cooking)

  1. In a heavy-bottomed pan, layer half the cooked rice.
  2. Spread the chicken gravy over the rice.
  3. Top with remaining rice.
  4. Sprinkle fried onions, saffron milk, and optional cashews/raisins.
  5. Cover tightly with a lid or foil.

Step 5: Cook on Dum

  1. Cook on very low heat for 20-25 minutes.
  2. Turn off the heat and let it rest for 10 minutes before opening.

Html Glossary

  1. Html
    2. HTML stands for HyperText Markup Language.HTML is the standard language used to create web pages. It tells the web browser how to structure and display content such as text, images, links, tables, and forms.
  2. Tag
    3. A tag is a keyword enclosed in angle brackets (< >). Tags are used to define and format content such as headings, paragraphs, images, and links.
  3. ELEMENT
    4. An HTML element consists of an opening tag, the content, and a closing tag. For example, a paragraph element includes and with text inside.
  4. ATTRIBUTE
    5. Attributes give additional information about an HTML element. They are written inside the opening tag and help control behavior, such as href in links or src in images.
  5. Browser
    6. A browser is a software application like Chrome or Firefox that reads HTML code and displays the web page in a user-friendly format.
